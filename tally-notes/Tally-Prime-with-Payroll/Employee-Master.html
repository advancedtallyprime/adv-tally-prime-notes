The "Why" (Real-Life Context)

Imagine a company with 500 employees. If you want to know the total salary spent only on the "Sales" department, you can't just look at a giant list of names.

  • Groups allow you to categorize employees (Marketing, HR, etc.).
  • Units define how you measure their work (Days, Hours).
  • Employee Master stores their "Digital File"—Bank details, PAN, and Aadhaar—which is mandatory for filing government returns.

Step 1: Create Employee Groups

This allows for department-wise reporting.

  1. Go to Gateway of Tally → Create → Payroll Masters → Employee Group.
  2. Create the following groups:
  • Marketing
  • Sales
  • IT
  • HR
  • Finance

Set Under: Primary for all.

Step 2: Create Units (Work)

Before you hire someone, you must decide how to count their time.

Simple Units

  1. Create → Payroll Masters → Unit (Work).
  2. Type: Simple | Symbol: Day | Formal Name: Number of Days
  3. Type: Simple | Symbol: Hrs | Formal Name: Hours

Compound Unit

  1. Type: Compound
  2. First Unit: Days
  3. Conversion: 8
  4. Second Unit: Hrs
  5. (Meaning: 1 Day = 8 Hours of work)

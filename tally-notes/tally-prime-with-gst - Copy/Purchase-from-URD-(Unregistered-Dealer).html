1. Theory – Purchase from Unregistered Dealer (URD)

Unregistered Dealer (URD)

Supplier not registered under GST
→ Cannot charge GST in invoice

Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM)

Recipient (you) pays GST directly to government
Notified services/goods only (e.g., GTA, Legal, Security, Sponsorship)

RCM Workflow (3 Steps)

  1. Record Purchase (No GST in invoice)
  2. Raise RCM Tax Liability (Output Tax)
  3. Claim ITC simultaneously (Input Tax Credit)

Net Cash Outflow = Zero (if ITC fully available)

2. Practical Setup – Company: ABC Traders (Regular Taxpayer)

Create URD Supplier Ledger

Name: URD Supplier - Legal Services
Under: Sundry Creditors
GST Applicable: Yes → Set/Alter GST Details
Registration Type: Unregistered

Required Tax Ledgers (Create if missing)

  • Input CGST / Input SGST
  • Output CGST / Output SGST
  • Taxes Payable RCM (Current Liabilities)

3. Step-by-Step Entries – Example: Legal Fees ₹5,000 + 18% RCM

Step 1: Purchase Voucher (F9)
Party: URD Supplier - Legal Services
Ledger: Legal Fees A/c Dr → ₹5,000
No GST charged by supplier
Step 2: RCM Liability Journal (F7 + Stat Adjustment)
Stat Adjustment → GST → Increase in Tax Liability → Purchase from URD
Dr Output CGST → ₹450
Dr Output SGST → ₹450
Cr Taxes Payable RCM → ₹900
Step 3: Claim ITC Journal (F7 + Stat Adjustment)
Increase of Input Tax Credit → Purchase from URD
Dr Input CGST → ₹450
Dr Input SGST → ₹450
Cr Taxes Payable RCM → ₹900
→ Ledger balance becomes zero

6. Assignment – Security Services (Monthly Contract)

Transaction: ₹25,000 on 30-Apr-2024
GST @18% = ₹4,500 (CGST ₹2,250 + SGST ₹2,250)

  1. Record Purchase Voucher (No GST)
  2. Pass RCM Liability Journal → ₹4,500
  3. Pass ITC Claim Journal → ₹4,500
  4. Verify in GSTR-3B:
    • 3.1(d) → ₹4,500 (Liability)
    • 4(A)(2) → ₹4,500 (ITC)
  5. Answer: Net Cash Outflow for GST = ₹0 (Fully neutral)

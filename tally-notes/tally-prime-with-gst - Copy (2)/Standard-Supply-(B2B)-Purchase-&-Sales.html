1. Theory – B2B Standard Supply

B2B Transactions

Supply between two GST-registered Regular Taxpayers
→ Full ITC available on purchases

ITC & Output Liability

ITC: Tax paid on buys (set-off against output)
Output: Tax collected on sales (pay to govt after ITC)

Tax Mapping – Intra vs Inter-State

TypeLocationTax Components
Intra-StateSame State (Maharashtra)CGST + SGST
Inter-StateDifferent States (Maharashtra → Goa)IGST

2. Practical Setup – Verify Party Ledgers

Gateway → Alter → Ledger → Local Supplier / Local Customer

  • Registration Type: Regular
  • State: Maharashtra
  • GSTIN: Valid 15-digit

For Inter-State: Set State to Goa (State Code 30)

3. Step-by-Step Entries – 4 Real Scenarios

Scenario A: Intra-State Purchase (ITC)

5 Laptops @ ₹30,000 = ₹1,50,000
F9 Purchase → Local Supplier
→ Input CGST ₹13,500 + Input SGST ₹13,500
Total: ₹1,77,000 | ITC: ₹27,000

Scenario B: Inter-State Purchase (ITC)

50 Mice @ ₹400 = ₹20,000
F9 Purchase → Inter-State Supplier (Goa)
→ Input IGST ₹3,600
Total: ₹23,600 | ITC: ₹3,600

Scenario C: Intra-State Sale (Liability)

2 Laptops @ ₹45,000 = ₹90,000
F8 Sales → Local Customer
→ Output CGST ₹8,100 + Output SGST ₹8,100
Total: ₹1,06,200 | Liability: ₹16,200

Scenario D: Inter-State Sale (Liability)

10 Mice @ ₹600 = ₹6,000
F8 Sales → Inter-State Customer (Goa)
→ Output IGST ₹1,080
Total: ₹7,080 | Liability: ₹1,080

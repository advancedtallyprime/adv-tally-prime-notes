The "Why" (Real-Life Context)

Day 6 is about moving from individual records to Bulk Reporting. While a Payslip is for the employee, the Pay Sheet and Attendance Register are for the management and auditors. These reports allow you to see the "Big Picture" of your company's monthly expenses.

  • Pay Sheet: Used for fund planning (e.g., "We need ₹5,00,000 in the bank by the 30th to cover all salaries").
  • Attendance Register: Used for auditing (e.g., "Why did the overtime cost double this month? Let's check who worked extra hours").

Step 1: The Pay Sheet (The "Master" Report)

The Pay Sheet displays all employees in rows and their various pay components (Basic, HRA, PF, etc.) in columns.

Navigation: Gateway of Tally → Display More Reports → Payroll Reports → Pay Sheet

  1. Select Group: View for "All Items" or specific departments like "Marketing."
  2. Configuration (F12): Enable "Detailed" view to see the breakdown of every single pay head.
  3. Analysis: Look at the bottom "Grand Total" line. This tells you exactly how much the company owes in salaries versus how much is being held as PF/ESI liabilities.

Step 2: The Attendance Register

This report tracks the "Time Input" of your workforce.

Navigation: Gateway of Tally → Display More Reports → Payroll Reports → Attendance Register

  1. Select the month (e.g., May).
  2. Tally shows a summary of days present, absent, and production (overtime) units.
  3. Pro-Tip: If you see a discrepancy in a salary, always check the Attendance Register first. It is the "Source of Truth" for salary calculations.

Step 3: Exporting and Printing

In real-life scenarios, the Pay Sheet is often exported to Excel for further analysis or shared with the Finance Department.

  1. While on the Pay Sheet, press Ctrl + E (Export).
  2. Select File Format: Excel (Spreadsheet).
  3. This allows you to create charts or pivot tables based on Tally's data.

1. Theory – Composite vs Mixed Supply

Composite Supply

Naturally bundled
One Principal Supply
→ Entire bundle taxed at Principal Rate

Example: Laptop + Free AMC → 18%

Mixed Supply

Not naturally bundled
Sold together for single price
→ Entire bundle taxed at Highest Rate

Example: Gift Box (Chocolates 12% + USB 18%) → 18%

3. Step-by-Step Entries

Scenario A: Composite Supply (Laptop + AMC)

Laptop (₹40,000) + 1-Year AMC (₹5,000) → Principal = Laptop → 18%

Sales Voucher (F8)
Laptop → ₹40,000
Service: AMC → ₹5,000
Total Value: ₹45,000
Tally applies: 18% → CGST ₹4,050 + SGST ₹4,050
Final Invoice: ₹53,100

Scenario B: Mixed Supply (Mouse + Laptop Bag)

Mouse (18%) + Laptop Bag (28%) → Sold as combo for ₹2,000

Best Method: Create item “Combo Pack” with 28% rate
OR manually override tax rate in voucher
Result: Entire ₹2,000 → 28% → CGST+SGST = ₹560

6. Assignment – Festival Combo Pack (Inter-State to Goa)

Package: ₹10,000 (Single Price)
Includes: Laptop Bag (28%) + AMC Service (18%)

  1. Is it Composite or Mixed?
    → Mixed Supply (not naturally bundled)
  2. Applicable Rate?
    → Highest = 28%

  4. GST Ledger Used?
    → Output IGST @ 28%
  5. IGST Amount?
    → ₹10,000 × 28% = ₹2,800
  6. Final Invoice Total?
    → ₹12,800

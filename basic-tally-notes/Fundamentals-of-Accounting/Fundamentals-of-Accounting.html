1. Introduction to Accounting

Definition: Accounting is the systematic process of recording, classifying, summarizing, and interpreting financial transactions to know profit/loss and financial position.

Why Needed?

  • Owner → “Am I making profit?”
  • Bank → Creditworthiness check
  • Government → GST, Income Tax
  • Management → Decision making

Real-Life Example: A mobile shop owner sells 50 phones. Without accounting, he won’t know whether he earned ₹50,000 profit or lost ₹20,000 after expenses.

4. Golden Rules of Accounting (Most Important)

Personal Account

Debit the Receiver
Credit the Giver

Ex: Ram pays us → Cr. Ram A/c

Real Account

Debit What Comes In
Credit What Goes Out

Ex: Furniture purchased → Dr. Furniture

Nominal Account

Debit All Expenses & Losses
Credit All Incomes & Gains

Ex: Paid Salary → Dr. Salary A/c

6. Trial Balance

Purpose: To check arithmetical accuracy (Total Debit = Total Credit)

In Tally: Gateway of Tally → Display More Reports → Trial Balance (Auto-generated)

7. Financial Statements

  1. Trading A/c → Gross Profit
  2. Profit & Loss A/c → Net Profit
  3. Balance Sheet → Financial Position (Assets = Capital + Liabilities)

In Tally: Gateway of Tally → Profit & Loss A/c | Balance Sheet (Auto)

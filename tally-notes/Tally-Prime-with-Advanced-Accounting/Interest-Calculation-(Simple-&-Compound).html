1. Introduction

Automatically calculate and charge interest on:

  • Overdue customer invoices
  • Delayed supplier payments
  • Employee loans (compound)
  • Bank loans & deposits

Tally calculates interest daily — you just record the entry!

2. Theory – Types of Interest

Simple Interest

Formula:
I = P × R × T / 100
(or /365 for daily)

Used for overdue bills

Compound Interest

Formula:
A = P(1 + R/100)n

Interest on interest
Used for loans

Bill-by-Bill Interest

Interest only on individual overdue invoices
Not on total balance

3. Enable Interest Calculation

F11 → Accounting Features →

  • Activate Interest Calculation → YES
  • Calculate Interest Transaction-by-Transaction → YES
  • Bill-wise Details must be ON

4. Configure Interest in Ledgers (Critical Step)

Customer1 (Sundry Debtors)
→ Activate Interest: YES
→ Rate: 18% p.a.
→ Style: Simple
→ On: Past Due Date (after 30 days)
Vendor1 (Sundry Creditors)
→ Rate: 12% p.a.
→ Simple Interest
→ On overdue bills
Employee Loan A/c
→ Rate: 10%
→ Style: Compound Monthly

5–6. Step-by-Step Examples

Example 1: Customer Overdue Interest
Invoice: ₹50,000 | Due: 30 days | Paid after 80 days
Overdue: 50 days | Rate: 18%
Interest = ₹50,000 × 18% × 50/365 ≈ ₹1,233
Tally shows exact amount automatically
Example 3: Compound Interest Loan
₹2,00,000 @ 10% compound monthly → 3 months
Final Amount ≈ ₹2,05,040
Interest = ₹5,040

7. Interest Reports – View & Record

Path:
Display → Statement of Accounts → Interest Calculations

  • Bill-wise overdue interest
  • Compound interest breakdown
  • Interest Receivable / Payable Summary

Then pass Journal Entry to record interest

The "Why" (Real-Life Context)

Day 7 marks the transition from internal accounting to Government Compliance. After paying your employees, the company must deposit the deducted amounts (PF, ESI, and PT) to the respective government departments.

Deducting PF from an employee's salary is only half the job. If the company fails to deposit this money with the EPFO or ESIC by the 15th of the following month, they face heavy penalties. Tally Prime generates the Challans and Computation reports automatically so you don't have to calculate them manually.

Step 1: PF (Provident Fund) Reports & Challans

Navigation: Display More Reports → Payroll Reports → Statutory Reports → Provident Fund

  • Combined Challan: Shows breakdown of Account No. 1 (Employee & Employer share), Account No. 2 (Admin charges), etc.
  • Monthly Statement: Use this to verify the PF amount for every employee before uploading to the PF portal.

Step 2: ESI (Employee State Insurance) Reports

Navigation: Display More Reports → Payroll Reports → Statutory Reports → Employee State Insurance

  • ESI Monthly Review: Lists Gross Wages, Employee’s 0.75% contribution, Employer’s 3.25% contribution (or your custom rate).
  • ESI Challan: Generates data required for ESIC portal payment.

Step 3: Professional Tax (PT)

Professional Tax is a state-level tax (e.g., slab-based flat rate in Maharashtra).

Report: Statutory Reports → Professional Tax → Computation Report

Step 4: Statutory Payment (The Accounting Entry)

  1. Go to Vouchers → F5 (Payment).
  2. Press Ctrl + F (Autofill).
    • Process For: Statutory Payment
    • Statutory Pay Type: PF (or ESI)
    • Period: 1-May to 31-May
  3. Tally auto-fills the amounts. Credit your Bank Account to complete.

Final Practice Set: The Monthly Closing

  1. Generate the PF Monthly Statement for May 2021.
  2. Confirm that the total PF amount is exactly 12% of the total "Basic Pay" shown in your Pay Sheet.
  3. The Final Challenge: Create a Payment Voucher to pay the total salary to all employees via Bank instead of Cash.

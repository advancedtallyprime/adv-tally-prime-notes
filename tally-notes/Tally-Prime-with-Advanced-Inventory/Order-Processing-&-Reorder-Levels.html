I. üìö Detailed Overview & Explanation (Theory)

Order Processing and Reorder Levels are essential components of effective Inventory Management. They ensure that the right quantity of goods is available at the right time, minimizing both stock-out situations and excessive capital tied up in inventory.

Order Processing

  • This feature allows a business to accurately track the movement of goods from the moment an order is placed until it is fulfilled.
  • It involves recording Sales Orders (orders received from customers) and Purchase Orders (orders placed with suppliers).
  • Tracking orders separately from actual delivery/receipt of goods helps in knowing the Commitments (what needs to be sent or received).

Reorder Level (ROL)

  • The Reorder Level is a threshold quantity set for a specific stock item. When the existing stock level falls to this quantity, a new order must be placed to replenish the inventory.
  • Formula: \( ROL = \text{Lead Time Consumption} + \text{Safety Stock} \)
    • Lead Time Consumption: Average consumption during the time it takes for a new order to arrive.
    • Safety Stock: Buffer stock to guard against unexpected demand or delays.
  • Use in TallyPrime: TallyPrime automatically suggests raising a Purchase Order when stock reaches the Reorder Level.

Reorder Quantity

The quantity to be ordered when the Reorder Level is reached.

Formula: \( \text{Reorder Quantity} = \text{Maximum Stock Level} - \text{Reorder Level} + \text{Consumption during Lead Time} \)

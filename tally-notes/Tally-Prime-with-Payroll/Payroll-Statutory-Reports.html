The "Why" (Real-Life Context)

Day 8 is the final step in the Payroll cycle: Audit and Reporting. After processing salaries and payments, you must generate the legal documents required for government audits and internal records. These reports prove that your company is compliant with Indian Labor Laws.

In a real company, if a government officer visits for an inspection, they won't ask for your Tally vouchers; they will ask for Form 12A (PF) or the ESI Register. These are standardized formats prescribed by the government. Tally Prime's "Statutory Reports" section automatically formats your data into these legal layouts.

1. PF (Provident Fund) Statutory Reports

Navigation: Display More Reports → Payroll Reports → Statutory Reports → Provident Fund

  • Form 5 & 10: For new joiners & leavers
  • Form 12A: Monthly report of wages & contributions
  • PF Monthly Statement: Detailed employee-wise PF contribution

2. ESI (Employee State Insurance) Reports

Navigation: Display More Reports → Payroll Reports → Statutory Reports → Employee State Insurance

  • ESI Form 3: Return of contributions
  • ESI Monthly Statement: Gross wages & contributions (Employee 0.75% + Employer 3.25%)

3. Income Tax (IT) Reports

Navigation: Display More Reports → Payroll Reports → Statutory Reports → Income Tax

  • Computation Report: TDS based on Old/New Regime
  • Form 24Q: Quarterly TDS return (upload to Income Tax portal)

4. Employee-Specific Statutory Reports

  • Passport/Visa/Contract Details (for MNCs)
  • PF Ledger – Personal PF accumulation history

Final Practice Checklist

  1. Check for "Unallocated" entries in PF Monthly Statement → Fix missing PF Account Number in Employee Master.
  2. Verify TAN in Income Tax reports → Re-enter in F11 if missing.
  3. Print Combined PF Challan (Ctrl + P) — Most requested by management.

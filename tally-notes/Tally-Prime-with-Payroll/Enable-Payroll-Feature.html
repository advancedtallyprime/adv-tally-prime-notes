The "Why" (Real-Life Context)

In a standard accounting setup, Tally only tracks "What" you spent (e.g., ₹10,000 on Salaries). By enabling Payroll, you tell Tally to track the "Who, How, and Why" (e.g., ₹10,000 spent because Rajesh worked 25 days, earned HRA, and contributed to PF).

Activating Statutory Compliance ensures that government-mandated deductions like Provident Fund (PF) and Employee State Insurance (ESI) are calculated based on current Indian labor laws.

Step 1: Enabling the Payroll Module

  1. Open your Company in Tally Prime.
  2. Press F11 (Features).
  3. Under the Payroll section:
    • Maintain Payroll: Set to Yes (unlocks Employee Masters).
    • Enable Payroll Statutory: Set to Yes (unlocks PF, ESI, Income Tax).

Step 3: Verification

After saving (Ctrl+A), return to Gateway of Tally. You should now see "Payroll Masters" under Create and Alter menus.

If not visible → Features not saved correctly.

Practical Pro-Tip

In a real office, the "Standard Working Days" for ESI (usually 26) is crucial. If an employee joins mid-month, Tally uses this number to calculate the daily rate for statutory deductions. Always double-check your HR policy before entering this number.

B. Theory Section – In-depth Explanation

1. Purchase Order (PO)

  • Definition: A Purchase Order is a formal document sent to a supplier requesting the supply of goods at agreed rates and quantities.
  • Purpose:
    • Confirms intention to purchase.
    • Helps manage inventory planning.
    • Locks the supplier rate and terms.
    • Acts as a reference for Goods Receipt Notes (GRN) and Purchase Invoices.
  • Accounting Impact:
    • No immediate ledger impact.
    • Inventory is not affected until goods are received (GRN).
  • Business Scenario: A company wants to purchase 50 chairs from a supplier. It sends a PO to confirm order details before delivery.

2. Sales Order (SO)

  • Definition: A Sales Order is a confirmation document sent to a customer for goods to be delivered at a specified date, quantity, and price.
  • Purpose:
    • Confirms sales commitments.
    • Helps in inventory planning and production scheduling.
    • Provides tracking of pending sales.
  • Accounting Impact:
    • No immediate ledger or inventory impact.
    • Inventory is updated when the Delivery Note or Invoice is created.
  • Business Scenario: A company receives an order from a customer for 20 laptops. An SO is created to confirm quantity, price, and delivery schedule.

3. Difference Between PO and SO

4. Voucher Flow / Lifecycle

Purchase Cycle:
PO → GRN (Goods Received Note) → Purchase Invoice → Payment

Sales Cycle:
SO → Delivery Note (DN) → Sales Invoice → Receipt

✅ PO & SO are planning vouchers, not accounting vouchers. They track commitments, not transactions.

5. Ledger & Stock Requirements

Before creating PO/SO:

  • Supplier Ledger or Customer Ledger must be created.
  • Stock Items must exist with proper units of measure.
  • Enable Inventory and Accounts Features in F11 if not already.

C. Activating Voucher Types in Tally Prime (Mandatory)

In new Tally Prime, PO & SO are not active by default. There are two ways to activate them:

Option 1 – Through Masters

  1. Gateway of Tally → Masters → Alter → Accounting Masters
  2. Select Voucher Type → Purchase Order
  3. Activate → Yes
  4. Repeat for Sales Order

Option 2 – Through Transactions

  1. Gateway of Tally → Transactions → Vouchers
  2. Press F10 / Other Vouchers → Show Inactive
  3. Select Inventory Vouchers → Purchase Order → Activate → Yes
  4. Repeat for Sales Order

✅ Tip: Always activate PO & SO before creating entries to avoid errors.

D. Practical Section – Step by Step with Details

1. Creating a Purchase Order

  1. Gateway of Tally → Inventory Vouchers → F9: Purchase Order
  2. Supplier Ledger: Select the supplier from whom you are ordering.
  3. Order Number: Enter a unique identifier, e.g., PO-001.
  4. Date: Enter order date.
  5. Stock Items:
    • Select items from the stock list.
    • Enter Quantity and Rate.
    • Total amount will auto-calculate.
  6. Narration: Optional description, e.g., “Ordering 5 laptops for office use.”
  7. Press Enter → Save

2. Creating a Sales Order

  1. Gateway of Tally → Inventory Vouchers → F8: Sales Order
  2. Customer Ledger: Select the customer.
  3. Order Number & Date: Fill appropriately.
  4. Stock Items: Add items, quantity, rate, and check total.
  5. Narration: Optional description, e.g., “Sale of 3 laptops to XYZ Traders.”
  6. Press Enter → Save

✅ Tip: Always check ledger, stock, and quantity before saving.

F. Common Mistakes & Tips

  1. Mistake: PO/SO not showing in voucher menu → Solution: Activate voucher type.
  2. Mistake: Wrong supplier/customer selected → Solution: Always verify ledger.
  3. Mistake: Quantity or rate mismatch → Solution: Double-check stock items before saving.
  4. Tip: Use meaningful Order No for tracking.
  5. Tip: Include narration for future reference.

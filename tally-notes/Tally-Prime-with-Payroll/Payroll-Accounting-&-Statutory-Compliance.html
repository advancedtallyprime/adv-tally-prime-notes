Welcome to Day 1 of your Tally Prime Payroll journey

Today, we focus on the "Brain" of the payroll system: Statutory Compliance. In the real world, payroll isn't just about giving a salary; it’s about following government laws like Provident Fund (PF), Employee State Insurance (ESI), and Income Tax.

1. Conceptual Understanding

In India, every business with a certain number of employees must follow these rules:

Act/StatuteWhat it is?Why is it important?
Provident Fund (PF)Social Security/SavingsA portion of salary is saved for the employee's retirement.
ESIHealth InsuranceProvides medical benefits to employees earning below a specific limit.
Income Tax (TDS)Tax on EarningsThe company must deduct tax (TDS) if the employee's income crosses the taxable limit.
Professional TaxState TaxA small tax paid to the State Government for the privilege of working.

2. Step-by-Step Practical Setup

Step A: Enable Payroll Features

  1. Go to Gateway of Tally → F11: Features.
  2. Under the Payroll section:
    • Set Maintain Payroll to Yes.
    • Set Enable Payroll Statutory to Yes.

Step B: Enter Statutory Details

Once Statutory is set to Yes, a sub-screen opens. Enter the following:

Provident Fund (PF)Company CodeMH/12345

Step C: Adding Opening Capital

Before paying salaries, the company needs money in its account.

Path: Gateway of Tally → Vouchers → F6: Receipt
Credit: Capital Account — ₹1,00,000
Debit: Cash — ₹1,00,000

4. Real-Life Example: The "Compliance First" Rule

Imagine you start a company called "Alpha Tech." You hire 5 people on the 1st of the month. If you haven't entered your TAN number in Tally on Day 1, Tally won't know which government department to credit the tax to when you generate the payslip at the end of the month.

Statutory Setup is a one-time activity, but it controls every calculation Tally makes later.

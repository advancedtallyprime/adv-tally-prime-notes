1. What is a Ledger? — Theory

A Ledger is the basic account in which all business transactions are recorded.
Examples: Cash, Bank, Sales, Purchase, Capital, GST, Loan, Rent, Salary etc.

Every ledger must belong to a Group.
Tally Prime comes with 28 predefined Groups.

2. Types of Ledger Groups

2.1 Primary Groups

  • Capital Account
  • Loans (Liability)
  • Current Assets / Current Liabilities
  • Fixed Assets
  • Sales / Purchase Accounts
  • Direct / Indirect Incomes & Expenses

2.2 Predefined Groups (28 Groups List)

  • Capital & Liability: Capital Account, Reserves & Surplus, Loans, Sundry Creditors, Duties & Taxes
  • Assets: Fixed Assets, Current Assets, Cash-in-hand, Bank Accounts, Sundry Debtors
  • Income: Sales Accounts, Direct Incomes, Indirect Incomes
  • Expenses: Purchase Accounts, Direct Expenses, Indirect Expenses

3. Ledger Creation (Theory)

Each ledger must have:

  1. Ledger Name
  2. Group (Decides behavior in reports)
  3. GST/Tax settings (Optional)
  4. Inventory values (Optional)
  5. Opening Balance (If any)

4. Practical: Steps to Create Ledger in Tally Prime

Method 1: Gateway → Create Master → Ledger

Method 2: During voucher entry → Type name → Alt + C → Create instantly

5. Practical Ledger Examples (All Important Groups)

5.1 Capital & Owner’s Equity

  • Capital → Group: Capital Account → Opening: ₹1,00,000

5.2 Asset Ledgers

  • Cash → Cash-in-hand
  • HDFC Bank → Bank Accounts
  • Computer → Fixed Assets → ₹45,000
  • ABC Traders → Sundry Debtors → Dr ₹20,000

5.3 Liability Ledgers

  • XYZ Suppliers → Sundry Creditors → Cr ₹30,000
  • CGST/SGST Payable → Duties & Taxes

5.4 Purchase & Sales

  • Sales @18% → Sales Accounts → GST: Yes
  • Purchase @18% → Purchase Accounts → GST: Yes

5.5 Expense & Income

  • Rent, Salary → Indirect Expenses
  • Wages → Direct Expenses
  • Interest Received → Indirect Incomes

5.6 GST Tax Ledgers

  • CGST 9%, SGST 9%, IGST 18% → Group: Duties & Taxes → Type: GST
